Wine Cooler Not Cooling: Common Reasons & How To Fix It

Most of these problems can be troubleshot at home, but a technician is needed for advanced issues.

Electricity

A break in electricity can be one of the causes why your wine cooler stops working. Although this is not a mechanical problem with the wine cooler, it is common. Before you check any mechanical issues, always check the main power supply.

Problems With The Compressor

A compressor that is not working will cause temperature problems. A reason for this could be that it is not receiving sufficient power, or it could be overheating, and that will cause it to malfunction and stop working altogether.

Problems With The Capacitor

Common problems with the capacitor could be that the unit is not receiving enough power to start the compressor. There could be a surge in electricity that causes the capacitor to burn out and seize up.

Problems With The Condenser Fan

If any of the condenser fan components stop working, it will cause the wine cooler to overheat. A fan blade can crack and break off, creating an uneven rotational cycle, and the fan can stop working. The other issue that can arise is that the teeth of the mechanism can strip, and the fan can no longer spin.

Problems With The Thermostat

A thermostat may become faulty if you experience many power surges or power outages. Usually, a faulty thermostat will either warm up the interior unit of the wine cooler or cool it well below the freezing point. This problem arises when one or more sensors malfunction sending incorrect signals to the thermostat. If you cannot rectify this problem immediately, the wine will spoil.

The Evaporator

Problems with the evaporator can be caused by a blockage in the line causing the unit to be warmer than it should be. The evaporator has a fan, and it can accumulate a lot of dust and oily debris if not cleaned regularly. The evaporator fan blades can bend and stop working.
